CHICKEN HALEEM

Chicken Haleem is a popular dish in Karachi, Pakistan, enjoyed during special occasions and religious festivals like Ramadan. It is a slow-cooked dish that combines shredded chicken, lentils, wheat, and spices to create a thick and porridge-like consistency. In Karachi, Chicken Haleem can be found at local eateries, street food stalls, and restaurants specializing in Pakistani cuisine. The dish is known for its rich and creamy texture, as well as its aromatic flavors from a blend of spices. Chicken Haleem is typically garnished with fried onions, fresh coriander leaves, ginger slices, and a squeeze of lemon. It is commonly served with naan bread or rice and provides a satisfying and comforting meal experience.
